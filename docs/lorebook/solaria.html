What is Solaria?

☉

The Lost Science of the Sun

Solaria is a form of cosmic energy discovered and harnessed by Mesoamerican civilizations, most notably the Tenochca, through a sophisticated system of engineering.

Originally, the Aztecs called it Tonatiuh, meaning “Movement of the Sun” in Nahuatl, referring to the full spectrum of cosmic energy, including both bright (light, heat, illumination) and void (darkness, cold, entropy) forces. To conceal the existence of this energy, Seekers told Europeans that Tonatiuh was a deity worshiped by the natives.

“What you call sorcery is but the science of antiquity — a science our instruments are not yet refined enough to discern.”

— Paracelsus

Opera Omnia Apocrypha (attrib.)

Nomenclature

This power goes by different names, depending on the culture interpreting it:

  • Solaria — Latin term derived from a literal translation of the term Tonatiuh; used by European Seekers, Inquisitors, and scholars.
  • Tonatiuh — Nahuatl term meaning “Movement of the Sun”; refers to the total cosmic energy including both bright and void aspects. Today, the term is recognized as the Aztec god of the day. This stems from a misinterpretation by the Buscadores: the Spanish thought that the Aztecs worshipped a deity, when in fact they worshipped solar energy itself.

Other terms (e.g., Xiuhtlalpan, Æther Solis, Tonalli) appear in certain manuscripts but are secondary.

Duality of Energy: Bright and Void

Solaria manifests as a dual energy system:

  • Bright — associated with sunlight, heat, life, and illumination.
  • Void — associated with darkness, cold, entropy, and the cosmic vacuum.

Being a cosmically pervasive energy, Solaria flows through all matter and phenomena: not only the Sun and planets, but also stars, galaxies, black holes, and dark matter. While Aztecs understood this as a single Tonatiuh, European alchemists and scholars (influenced by Christianity) separated it conceptually. Void energy was considered occult, negative, even demonic, so the Papal Inquisitors were forbidden from using it.

How Does Solaria Work?

w

Placeholder

Solaria functions through a massive, continent-spanning network constructed by the ancient civilizations of the New World. This system, often mistaken by European scholars for mere ceremonial architecture, is in fact a precise system of energy capture, storage, and transmission — engineered to harness solar power and render it accessible to those attuned to it.

The Tenochca Solar Network

At the heart of this system are the great pyramids, whose stepped geometry and mineral composition allow them to capture and focus solar radiation. During the day, especially at dawn and dusk when the global lighting is dim, one can witness radiant beams of concentrated light descending from the sky onto the apex of these structures — a spectacle known to local cultures as the “Descent of the Sun Serpent.” These visible phenomena are not symbolic, but physical: they mark the active transmission of solar energy into the network.

From the pyramids, this energy is channeled into subterranean cenotes, deep sinkholes whose mineral-rich waters serve as natural capacitors. These cenotes glow with blinding intensity when charged, their surfaces shimmering with contained power. The water becomes lethally energized: a single touch often results in immediate death, as if one had grasped a live wire. Accessing such sites is strictly controlled by local priesthoods and wardens, who maintain the balance between ritual sanctity and technological necessity.

Once charged, the energy flows outward along ley-lines — artificial veins embedded across the land using conductive materials such as obsidian, copper, and jade. These lines connect pyramids, cenotes, and other key sites in a geometric grid, allowing energy to travel vast distances without dissipation. At intervals along this grid stand stele and obelisk-nodes, which act as field repeaters they rebroadcast the signal, stabilize local fluctuations, and enable attuned individuals to tap into the Solarian current with precision and control.

Users

Access & Manipulation

Practitioners draw upon the field through reactive materials and formulae.

Once within a broadcast zone, casters can access Solaria to invoke phenomena, cast spells, or manipulate the reality.

The Old World lacks this infrastructure entirely. Its megaliths, temples, and natural formations — while impressive — were never designed for energetic transmission. Without pyramidic collectors, charged cenotes, ley-lines, or calibrated obelisks, Solaria cannot manifest. No ritual, incantation, or relic is sufficient on its own: the field requires a living circuit — a continuous, functional system rooted in the land itself.

Modulation Mechanisms

To produce tangible effects, the field must be modulated according to precise patterns — formulas that disturb the field locally. Those patterns require:

  • Reactive Materials — Sacred substances such as obsidian, cenote water, and gold react to Solaria. Their sacredness originates from their natural responsiveness, enabling effects. They can be used as artifacts or incorporated into tattoos, body paint, incisions and glyphs on practitioners to enhance or even enable their ability to manipulate Solaria.
  • Spatial and Temporal Arrangement — Moving reactive materials through space and time in specific patterns disturbs the field at targeted points, producing desired phenomena.

Interfacing with Solaria

To become part of the circuit and interact with the field, practitioners use reactive materials on—and within—the body.

Mesoamerican Tradition: Tlāltikpak — Glyphs drawn or etched along muscular lines and joints with mineral inks and metallic paints (e.g., Maya Blue, red ochre, jade powders, gold/copper suspensions). These glyphic circuits couple the body to Solaria’s resonance. New World physiology—blood rich in iron and other reactive elements—provides baseline coupling, but major effects still require external artifacts and machines in sacred materials.

European Tradition: Imprinta Sigillata — An alchemical counterpart developed after studying Tlāltikpak: fine-line sigils and Hermetic diagrams inscribed with solar metals and infused blood (oxidized gold, verdigris copper, burnished iron). Activation typically requires celestial alignment and personal sacrifice.

Ritual Blood Use

Aztec priest-engineers historically distilled the blood of low-aptitude individuals to produce highly reactive serum distributed to citizens to augment coupling with the field. Seekers emulate this via transfusions or ingestion for minor effects; major workings still depend on sacred artifacts and machinery.

Who Can Use Solaria?

Anyone within the active Solaria field generated by the ancient network and possessing reactive materials (either as artifacts or within their body) can use Solaria, provided they know the proper techniques.

What are Solarian Effects?

w

Placeholder

After being disturbed by an individual interacting with it, the field tends to return to a state of equilibrium. In doing so, the energy passing through matter on its return journey generates a tangible physical phenomenon. Greater disturbances produce stronger effects.

Core Solarian physical phenomena include:

  • Gravitation — manipulation of gravitational forces for levitation, attraction, or propulsion.
  • Flare — instantaneous bursts of energy, light, or void explosions.
  • Beam — sustained energy rays or currents, luminous or void.
  • Mutation — permanent alteration of matter or living beings, enhancing or damaging.
  • Resonance — temporary disturbance of matter or living beings, weakening or confusing without permanent damage.

These are the core building blocks of Solaria’s effects. Many practical uses involve combinations of two or more effects, either in parallel (occurring simultaneously) or in series (occurring sequentially), following principles similar to modern electrical circuits.

Color Code & Aesthetics

Every use of Solaria leaves tangible, sensory traces in the world. It is a cosmic force — not subtle, not hidden. Where it flows, it bends the very air and matter around it, announcing its presence in ways both wondrous and undeniable.

Aspects of Solaria

g

Placeholder

Among those who have been altered by the Solaria field, patterns have emerged — recurring bonds between the Seeker’s will and specific manifestations of primal force. These patterns are known as Aspects.

Each Aspect resonates with a particular archetypal creature — not merely symbolic, but biologically and spiritually attuned to the pulse of Solaria. They represent an arcane synthesis: a deliberate attempt by the ancient Mesoamerican magi to engineer power by encoding the virtues of beasts into human form.

Aspects do not grant shapeshifting or bestial features (though some mutations are common), but rather influence how the Seeker channels magical energy, reacts to stimuli, and alters the world through Solaria.
