Personal Augmentations in a Shared Grid

In Seeker, combat is a structured interplay of turns, Stances, and strategic interventions. Each participant acts according to clearly defined rules, but the system is designed to encourage interaction beyond the individual turn: combatants can interrupt an opponent’s action, enhance an ally’s ability, modify the outcome of a pending effect, or trigger environmental or status-driven conditions at almost any moment.

Combat revolves around Stances, which determine not only the number of Actions available and the Tags that can be used, but also the order in which participants act. The battlefield is divided into Units, providing a flexible spatial framework for movement, range, and positioning, while environmental effects and Difficult Terrain further shape tactical decisions.

Active defense ensures that every Attack must be met with a counter, and the Escalate mechanic introduces a dynamic layer of risk and resource management. Together, these elements create a system that rewards foresight, timing, and creative use of abilities, transforming each encounter into a puzzle of strategy and adaptability.
