Core Concept

b

Freedom of Expression Beyond Archetypes

In Seeker, Skills are not predefined packages or rigid class abilities. They are the building blocks of personal expression, shaping both the tactical identity and the narrative voice of every character. Instead of following predetermined archetypes or fixed progression paths, each Seeker is free to assemble, adapt, and refine their Skill set in ways that reflect their own approach to survival, conflict, and discovery.

This design moves away from the traditional model of character growth in role-playing games, where choices are often channeled into narrow tracks or archetypal roles. In Seeker, no two characters are bound to resemble one another unless their players wish it. A Skill can be honed into precision, bent into strange new uses, or left as a versatile tool among many — allowing Seekers to evolve organically, responding to the world and their own emerging story.

By emphasizing modularity, adaptability, and open-ended design, Skills embody the spirit of exploration at the heart of Seeker. They are the instruments through which players carve their own path, ensuring that progression is not a ladder to climb, but a map of endless directions.

Skill Anatomy

a

Freedom of Expression Beyond Archetypes

In Seeker, a Skill is never just a flat ability or a static number — it is a living framework that defines how a character exerts their will upon the world. Each Skill is built from several interlocking elements that combine clarity, flexibility, and narrative resonance. By understanding its anatomy, both players and game masters can see how every Skill is designed to grow, adapt, and express a Seeker’s unique journey.

Three Structural Layers

Every Skill in Seeker is composed of three interconnected layers that work together to provide both reliability and room for growth. These layers give each Skill its identity while leaving space for personal customization and narrative depth.

Layer What it Represents
Core Effect The baseline action or benefit that the Skill always provides — the essential function that anchors its identity.
Augmentations Modular upgrades that refine, expand, or transform the Skill, offering new tactical choices, conditional triggers, or specialized mastery.
Tags Narrative descriptors that mark how the Skill interacts with the fiction — whether it manifests physically, socially, mystically, or in hybrid forms.

How the Layers Work

While each layer has its own role, their interaction is what makes a Skill dynamic. Core Effects ensure usability, Augmentations drive evolution, and Tags keep the Skill grounded in the fiction — together they form a flexible yet coherent system that adapts to every Seeker’s journey.

Core Effect

The Core Effect ensures that even a newly acquired Skill is immediately usable and meaningful in play. It defines the action’s reliable floor — the thing a Seeker can always fall back on.

Augmentations

Augmentations represent growth and differentiation. They allow the same Skill to look very different from character to character, evolving in tune with playstyle and narrative arc. Some augmentations sharpen the Skill’s efficiency, others bend its use into unexpected domains, and still others unlock entirely new dimensions of play.

Tags

Tags act as connective tissue between rules and fiction. They give context for rulings, help categorize interactions (e.g., when a mystical barrier blocks only “physical” Skills), and inspire narrative interpretation. Tags ensure that a Skill never exists in isolation but is always grounded in the world of Seeker.

Putting It Together

This tripartite design ensures that Skills are not only mechanically balanced but also expressive storytelling tools. A Skill like Tracking may begin as a straightforward Core Effect — following footprints across unfamiliar terrain — but through Augmentations it can evolve into reading subtle signs of supernatural presence or anticipating an enemy’s movements in combat. Its Tags anchor it as both physical and inquisitive, shaping how other systems interact with it.

Consistency & Expansion

By maintaining this consistent anatomy, Seeker achieves two goals: clarity for players, who can easily parse any new Skill they encounter, and design space for creators, who can confidently expand the Skill library without breaking system cohesion. Whether a Skill is simple and universal (like climbing or persuasion) or esoteric and rare (like dreamwalking or blood-binding), it follows the same structural blueprint — making the system scalable, modular, and endlessly expandable.

Augmentations

j

Freedom of Expression Beyond Archetypes

An Augmentation is a modular upgrade that expands or reshapes the way a Skill works. Unlike the core definition of the Skill — its Aptitude, its basic function, and its primary Tags — an Augmentation is something added later, as the Seeker grows. It represents specialization, mastery, or the discovery of a unique twist that makes a familiar technique entirely new.

Each Augmentation occupies a slot within the Skill and provides one or more effects. These effects can take many forms:

  • Enhancements that make the Skill more reliable or powerful.
  • Variants that change its behavior, turning a defensive Skill into a counterattack or a ranged attack into an area effect.
  • Extensions that add new Tags, interactions, or conditions, opening up further combinations with other Skills and Augmentations.

Importantly, Augmentations don’t just “stack” as passive bonuses. They alter the Skill’s identity: sometimes subtly, sometimes dramatically. A basic sword slash might gain bleeding properties, become infused with Solaria, or be redefined as a precise strike against a specific kind of foe.

In short, Skills are the foundation, while Augmentations are the architecture built on top — the flexible, evolving pieces that let Seekers adapt their abilities to their story, their playstyle, and the challenges of the New World.

Applying an Augmentation — Step by Step

When a Seeker gains an Augmentation, it must be applied to a specific Skill. This isn’t just a matter of attaching a bonus — it’s a structured process that ensures the new element fits the Skill’s identity, respects its internal logic, and doesn’t break the system’s balance.

The application of an Augmentation follows a simple but precise flow:

I • Choose the Skill

First, decide which Skill the Augmentation will modify. Each Skill has a limited number of Augmentation slots, and each slot may hold only one Augmentation at a time.

II • Simulate the Result

Before locking in the choice, you must check what the Skill would look like if this Augmentation were applied. This process is called simulation: the system temporarily combines the base Skill with all its currently assigned Augmentations — including the new one you are trying to add — and produces the resulting version of the Skill.

This simulated version is what determines whether the Augmentation is valid. If you are using the online tool, the simulation happens automatically. If you are playing on paper, it is strongly recommended that you reproduce the same process manually: apply the Augmentation to the Skill, check how it changes Tags, effects, or exclusions, and verify that no rules are broken before confirming the choice.

III • Check Compatibility

Not all Augmentations can coexist. Some explicitly exclude others (for example, two mutually exclusive upgrades), and others require that the Skill have certain Tags to make sense. These Tags might be added by the Skill itself or by other Augmentations. When checking a new Augmentation, you must verify that:

  1. It doesn’t conflict with any other Augmentation already assigned.
  2. Its required Tags are actually present in the simulated Skill (after all current and new effects have been applied).
  3. And it’s not already assigned elsewhere on the same Skill.

IV • Resolve Invalid States

If adding the Augmentation would violate one of the system’s conditions, it simply cannot be applied.

The system or tool will indicate why the choice is invalid: perhaps the Skill does not yet have the required Tags, or the new Augmentation conflicts with one already assigned. In these cases, you can either select a different Augmentation, or adjust the Skill’s build until the requirements are satisfied.

When playing on paper, it is particularly important to double-check the compatibility of all Augmentations before confirming them. This ensures that your Skill respects the rules and functions correctly during gameplay, avoiding inconsistencies or invalid combinations that could interfere with play.

V • Confirm and Recalculate

Once an Augmentation has passed all checks, it is formally assigned to the chosen slot. From this moment, its effects are integrated into the Skill — it may alter Tags, modify damage, add new conditions or effects, or even unlock entirely new options.

Each time an Augmentation is added, removed, or swapped, the Skill is recalculated from the ground up: all current Augmentations are reapplied in order, their effects are merged and aggregated (for example, multiple Augmentations adding the same Affliction will stack), and the Skill’s final Tags, effects, and properties are updated accordingly.

This dynamic rebuilding ensures that every Skill remains consistent, fully functional, and narratively coherent, providing a valid and playable expression of your character’s evolving growth.
