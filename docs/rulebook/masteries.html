Core Concept

b

Personal Augmentations in a Shared Grid

Masteries are not simple bonuses or passive traits — they are character augmentations, shaping the way each Seeker interacts with the world and redefining the limits of their abilities. Where Skills provide the raw expression of talent, Masteries serve as the framework of specialization, a strategic layer that empowers players to bend or reinforce their playstyle.

Each Seeker possesses a limited grid of hexagonal slots, a field of choices that reflects the tone of the campaign: sparse for grounded, realistic play, or expanded for more heroic, superhuman characters. Within this grid, Masteries are placed like tesserae, forming clusters of power, synergy, and identity. No two Seekers will ever arrange their grid in the same way, ensuring individuality at both the mechanical and narrative level.

To balance freedom with challenge, Masteries are bound by categorical anchors — Combat, Exploration, and Social — each carrying its own color identity and adjacency requirements. Some Masteries flourish only beside their kin, others demand a precise counterpart, and a few stand as wildcards, free to fit anywhere. This puzzle-like structure makes the act of building a character as much a game of strategy and foresight as one of imagination.

By combining modularity, constraint-driven creativity, and emergent synergy, Masteries embody Seeker’s core ethos: exploration not just of the New World, but of the self. They turn character growth into a living map, where every placement is both a choice and a discovery.

Section II

a

Mastery Anatomy

a

Identity Through Spatial Constraints

In Seeker, Masteries are more than perks or upgrades. They are structural anchors that define a character’s long-term trajectory, placed within a limited-slot hex grid. Their anatomy balances freedom of expression with spatial and categorical constraints, ensuring that each build feels both unique and earned.

Five Structural Elements

Every Mastery in Seeker is described using five interconnected elements that govern its identity and placement.

Element What it Represents
Category Its thematic domain: Combat, Exploration, or Social.
Core Effect The permanent benefit or defining function of the Mastery.
Placement Requirements Adjacency or positioning conditions on the hex board.
Character Prerequisites Requirements tied to the character’s state, aptitudes, or milestones.
Tags Descriptors that signal intent, role, or narrative resonance.

How the Elements Work

While each element serves a distinct function, it’s their interaction that makes a Mastery board dynamic: Categories frame identity, Placement & Adjacency shape connections, Slot Budget sets pacing, Character Prerequisites gate access, and Tags clarify intent — together they turn the grid into a coherent, evolving build space.

Categories

Categories provide the broad thematic axis — Combat (red), Exploration (blue), and Social (yellow). Each slot on the hex board exposes borders of one or more colors, and a Mastery may only be placed if at least one of its Categories matches an adjacent border.

Core Effect

The Core Effect is the heart of the Mastery. Unlike Skills, which evolve through Augmentations, a Mastery’s effect is stable and defining, often shifting the strategic identity of the character in a lasting way.

Placement Requirements

Placement rules create spatial tension on the board. Some Masteries demand adjacency to a specific Category (“Adjacent to two Exploration Masteries”), while others require proximity to a named Mastery (“Must touch Combat Adept”). A few rare Masteries act as wildcards, with no restrictions.

Character Prerequisites

Beyond the board, some Masteries require that the Seeker themselves has reached certain thresholds. These may include minimum Aptitude scores, prior Tags, or narrative milestones such as earning faction allegiance. This ensures that progression feels earned in the story, not just slotted onto the grid.

Tags

Tags are lightweight markers that clarify function and synergy. A Mastery with the Healer tag signals restorative play, while one tagged Scout leans toward mobility and reconnaissance. Tags help both rulings and narrative framing without introducing modular subsystems.

Putting It Together

These five elements combine to make Masteries both expressive and bounded. The hex board imposes structural limits, Categories and Placement Requirements shape spatial logic, Character Prerequisites tie choices to in-world growth, and Tags give narrative texture. A progression like Combat Adept → Combat Master is not just an increase in numbers but a deliberate, spatially constrained evolution that feels as much part of the fiction as of the system.

Consistency & Expansion

By maintaining this consistent anatomy, Seeker ensures that new Masteries can be added without breaking coherence. Whether a Mastery is basic and broad or esoteric and specialized, it always follows the same blueprint — keeping the system modular, scalable, and narratively grounded.

The Mastery Board

Masteries are placed on a hex grid with a fixed number of slots. Slot count acts as a difficulty/realism dial: more slots produce “super-heroic” breadth; fewer slots enforce grounded specialization. Each hex shows one or more colored borders: a Mastery can only be placed in a hex that touches a border matching at least one of its Categories.

Spatial Logic in Play

  • Color-gated placement: Category ↔ border color must match.
  • Adjacency validation: When placing a Mastery, its Placement Requirements must be met by neighboring hexes (by category count and/or specific Mastery IDs).
  • Multi-category Masteries: A Mastery with multiple Categories can satisfy multiple adjacency checks and can enter any slot whose border matches any of its Categories.
  • Wildcards: Some Masteries waive adjacency rules to enable flexible anchors for a build.

Design Goals

The anatomy above ensures Masteries deliver identity and constraint at once. The color-gated grid prevents runaway combinations, while adjacency requirements create deliberate, spatially meaningful builds that feel earned rather than piled on.
