To the brave and the curious,

I write you these words not as a scholar speaking to his peers, but as a man who has seen too much to remain silent and yet too little to claim certainty. My name, for now, shall be Alonso de Luna. It is not the name written on my passport, but it is the one I have chosen to protect those close to me ‚Äî and perhaps also to protect what little remains of my peace.

I come from a family whose name once opened doors in Madrid, Sevilla, even Lima, in the days when the world was still being drawn on parchment. Among old documents, family relics, and items that nobody had touched for generations, I found a fragment. It was not large, but it had a weight I cannot describe. Encased in dark volcanic glass, it held the faint trace of ink and salt, and something else. Una presencia.

From that moment, I could not rest. I began a journey, not just through places, but through meanings. The kind of path that does not lead forward, but downward, like a well with no bottom. From forgotten libraries to convents where the books are kept behind iron bars, I followed the signs until Rome, where the final door opened, not to light, but to a deeper darkness.

Inside the Secret Archives, in a section unmarked and almost erased from memory, I found documents that do not belong to the history we are taught. Letters from men who never returned. Sketches of cities not made by hands. Accounts of voices in the forest, and maps that shift when the candlelight flickers. Things that our world has chosen not to remember ‚Äî or perhaps, has been told to forget.

It became clear to me that I could not publish these findings in any academic journal. I do not have powerful friends. I am just a man who followed what he believed to be true. So I decided to hide my research inside a game. Not because it is a joke, but because games, like dreams, are the only places where the truth can still disguise itself and survive.

Seeker is the result of this decision. It is not fantasy, although it may appear so at first. It is a reconstruction, incomplete and fragile, based on the real materials I was able to collect. Some parts I had to invent, not to deceive, but to fill the silence left by time ‚Äî the silencio antiguo that still surrounds these testimonies.

If you choose to read it, or to play it, I ask you only one thing: pay attention. Sometimes, between one rule and the next, you may find something that does not belong entirely to fiction.

And if you do‚Ä¶ por favor, do not look away too quickly.

With quiet urgency,

No member of the team behind this project is a native English speaker or trained software developer. Using AI is the only accessible way to maintain a reasonable standard of linguistic and technical quality while focusing on game design, historical research, and storytelling.

AI is used for:

  • Translating and editing bilingual content (Spanish ‚Üî English)
  • Simulating game mechanics and hypothetical playtest scenarios
  • Researching historical sources and documentation
  • Structuring, summarizing, and analyzing complex design material
  • Editing, rewriting, and maintaining a consistent style across documents

AI is not used for:

  • Original creative writing (lore, adventures, character creation)
  • Generating visual art, concept pieces, or maps
  • Automatic name or language generation
  • Major editorial decisions or game design choices

All narrative content and visual material presented on this site are created by human authors, even when assisted by AI for revision or formatting purposes.

The goal is to use AI as a tool for research, development, and clarity ‚Äî while preserving creative integrity and authorial responsibility.
