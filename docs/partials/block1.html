Core Concept ‚∏∫ ‚ú¶ ‚∏∫

In Seeker, combat is not governed by random initiative rolls or rigid turn orders. Instead, each round begins with a deliberate choice: the Stance.

A Stance represents a character‚Äôs tactical posture ‚Äî their mindset and readiness in the face of conflict. It dictates how fast they act, what kind of skills they can use, and how effectively they can respond to threats.

Each Stance is more than a mechanical category: it reflects the character's immediate priorities, whether it's charging into battle, defending allies, repositioning, observing the field, or channeling arcane forces. Committing to a Stance means embracing both its advantages and its limitations.

What Are Stances?

At the beginning of each combat round, every character selects a Stance. A Stance defines your posture in the conflict ‚Äî whether you're striking first, playing defensively, or focusing on magic, positioning, or observation.

Your chosen Stance determines:

  • How many Actions you receive this round
  • Which Tags your Skills must have to be usable
  • Your position in the turn order, based on stance priority

Each Stance supports a different combat role. Choose one that aligns with your intentions for the round ‚Äî offense, defense, movement, support, magic, or versatility.

Aggressive Stance

The stance of the warrior who strikes first and hard, aiming to overwhelm opponents with relentless offense. This posture embodies direct confrontation, power, and high-impact tactics.

Actions & Allowed Tags

Actions: 8 Actions per round
Allowed Tags: Attack, Maneuver

Tactical Notes
‚ñ∏

The Aggressive Stance is best used when you want to seize initiative and apply immediate pressure on your foes. It favors players who prefer to dictate the pace of combat, aiming to disable or eliminate high-priority targets early.

In a system where attacks are only avoided through active defense, this stance shines when used against foes who are out of position, low on Action Dice, or locked in slower postures. With 8 Actions at your disposal, you can launch powerful opening strikes, escalate when challenged, and keep your enemies off-balance with relentless offense.

Strengths
‚ñ∏

These are the main strengths of this stance:

  • Highest priority in turn order, allowing you to act before most opponents
  • Full access to 8 Action Dice for offensive skill checks and escalation
  • Excellent for burst damage and disrupting enemy strategies before they can react
Limitations
‚ñ∏

These are the key limitations and trade-offs to consider:

  • No access to Defense or Support tags ‚Äî if attacked, you cannot actively defend
  • Vulnerable to counterattacks, especially if initiative advantage is lost
  • Relies on eliminating threats quickly before retaliation becomes dangerous
Synergies
‚ñ∏

These synergies help maximize the stance‚Äôs effectiveness:

  • Works well with high-impact or escalating Attack skills that force opponents to spend resources early
  • Pairs effectively with allies in Defensive or Support Stances who can cover your vulnerabilities
  • Ideal for characters with high Strength, Agility, or Intellect focused on offensive Aptitudes

Defensive Stance

The stance of guardians and tacticians. Defensive Stance centers on protecting yourself and your allies, resisting enemy blows, and creating windows of safety during chaotic encounters.

Actions & Allowed Tags

Actions: 8 Actions per round
Allowed Tags: Defense, Support

Tactical Notes
‚ñ∏

Defensive Stance is ideal for players who want to absorb aggression, counter enemy momentum, and control the tempo of the fight by denying opponents easy openings. In a system where defense requires deliberate investment, this stance is often the difference between survival and defeat.

It allows full access to Defense skills for intercepting or nullifying incoming attacks and Support skills for healing, shielding, or granting advantages to allies. Whether you're standing your ground or anchoring your team, this stance provides both durability and utility.

Strengths
‚ñ∏

These are the main strengths of this stance:

  • Grants full access to active defense, crucial for surviving strong enemy attacks
  • Supports escalation in defensive contests to hold the line when it matters most
  • Allows the use of powerful Support skills to heal or protect teammates mid-battle
Limitations
‚ñ∏

These are the key limitations and trade-offs to consider:

  • No access to Attack or Maneuver tags ‚Äî you cannot initiate direct offensive actions
  • Lower priority in turn order makes you react rather than lead
  • May feel passive if no allies require support and no attacks target you
Synergies
‚ñ∏

These synergies help maximize the stance‚Äôs effectiveness:

  • Excellent for tank-style characters who can draw aggression and hold ground
  • Pairs naturally with Aggressive and Mystic allies by covering their weaknesses
  • Well-suited for characters with high Vigor, Willpower, or Insight

Evasive Stance

The stance of nimble combatants who prioritize mobility and avoidance. Evasive Stance is about dodging danger, repositioning strategically, and frustrating enemy attacks through agility and clever movement.

Allowed Tags: Movement, Evasion, Defense

Tactical Notes
‚ñ∏

Evasive Stance excels at staying out of harm‚Äôs way, slipping past enemy lines, and forcing foes to waste actions trying to pin you down. It is ideal for characters who rely on agility and cunning to survive and create openings.

With fewer Actions than Aggressive or Defensive stances, careful planning is needed. Balancing between movement, defensive responses, and positioning can turn the tide by controlling the battlefield‚Äôs flow.

Strengths
‚ñ∏

These are the main strengths of this stance:

  • Allows active defense with fewer Actions, making you harder to hit
  • Enables quick repositioning and tactical retreats or flanking
  • Good synergy with hit-and-run or guerrilla tactics
Limitations
‚ñ∏

These are the key limitations and trade-offs to consider:

  • Fewer Actions overall limits the number of skills used per turn
  • Lower turn order priority than Aggressive stance

Synergies
‚ñ∏

These synergies help maximize the stance‚Äôs effectiveness:

Inquisitive Stance

The stance of the tactician, the scout, the scholar on the battlefield. Inquisitive Stance is about understanding the battlefield and exploiting information‚Äîdetecting enemy weaknesses, predicting their moves, or leveraging terrain and opportunity.

Balanced Stance

The stance of the adaptable and cautious. Balanced Stance grants you flexibility in your actions, allowing you to respond to developing threats without overcommitting to a single strategy. It favors those who thrive in uncertainty.

‚ñ∏

Balanced Stance is the ideal choice when you're uncertain about how a combat round will unfold. It grants you access to all skill types, which makes it a versatile fallback in unpredictable scenarios or multi-layered encounters.

Its drawback is the inefficiency in combat specialization: using Attack or Defense skills costs more Actions, making it less suited for direct duels or intensive confrontations. Still, its broad utility makes it a powerful stance for improvisational play and mixed strategies.

Strengths
‚ñ∏

These are the main strengths of this stance:

These are the key limitations and trade-offs to consider:

Basic and Specialized Stances

The six Stances listed below ‚Äî Aggressive, Defensive, Evasive, Inquisitive, Balanced, and Mystic ‚Äî are known as Basic Stances. These are universally available to all characters at all times and form the foundation of Seeker's stance-based initiative system.

As your character grows and learns new Skills and Masteries, they may gain access to Specialized Stances: highly focused combat postures that offer unique tactical advantages, expanded tag access, or special turn order rules.

Each Specialized Stance clearly defines:

  • Its granted Actions and allowed Tags
  • Any unique rules or restrictions it applies
  • Its turn order priority ‚Äî for example, Berserk may act before all other Stances, while Ambush may act before Aggressive but after Berserk

Refer to the description of each specific Skill or Mastery to learn how to unlock and use these powerful alternate Stances.

Section III

Initiative Order ‚∏∫ ‚ú¶ ‚∏∫

At the beginning of every combat round, all participants ‚Äî both PCs and NPCs ‚Äî simultaneously choose a Stance. This decision isn't made lightly: it sets the rhythm for everything that follows.

Your chosen Stance determines:

  • Your initiative priority ‚Äî who acts before whom
  • Which Tags you can use ‚Äî what kinds of skills are available to you this round
  • How many Actions you can spend ‚Äî and on which types of abilities
  • Whether you can defend yourself actively ‚Äî some Stances leave you vulnerable

This system replaces traditional initiative rolls with a tactical layer that rewards foresight, coordination, and risk assessment. Choosing a fast stance lets you strike first, but often sacrifices stance. Choosing a slow or reactive stance allows you to observe, support, or shield others ‚Äî but it might mean taking the first blow.

Rather than reacting to chance, players must anticipate threats, read the battlefield, and commit to a course of action before the first move is even made.

Resolving Turns

Once all characters have declared their Stance, the GM resolves turns by calling each Stance in a fixed priority order. Multiple characters in the same Stance act together when it‚Äôs called.

1. Aggressive

Characters in Aggressive Stance act first in the round. This stance reflects a mindset focused on seizing the initiative, launching decisive and overwhelming attacks to disrupt enemy plans before they can react. When the GM calls "Aggressive Stance!", these players take their turns with priority, aiming to shape the battlefield through bold and direct action.

2. Evasive

Next up are the characters in Evasive Stance. This stance emphasizes agility and avoidance, allowing these players to reposition, evade incoming threats, or counterattack with precision. When "Evasive Stance!" is called, they respond quickly to shifting circumstances, aiming to maintain control through flexibility and swift reactions.

3. Balanced

The Balanced Stance represents a steady, adaptable approach, combining offense, defense, and utility. Acting in the middle of the round, these characters adjust their tactics based on the flow of combat, ready to support allies, respond to threats, or press an advantage as the situation demands.

4. Mystic

The Mystic Stance grants spellcasters and arcane users their moment to weave magic into the battle. Acting after more physical stances, mystics carefully time their rituals and enchantments, influencing the battlefield with powerful effects, protections, or manipulations when the opportunity arises.

5. Defensive

Guardians and protectors in the Defensive Stance act next. This stance prioritizes shielding allies, absorbing blows, and holding positions. Defenders react with precision to enemy attacks, setting up protective measures and ensuring the team‚Äôs safety as the round progresses.

6. Inquisitive

Finally, the Inquisitive Stance concludes the round. Characters who rely on observation, investigation, and tactical insight act last, using fresh information gathered during the round to make informed decisions, exploit openings, or uncover hidden truths before the next turn cycle begins.

Tiebreakers Within the Same Stance

Sometimes, multiple characters will act within the same Stance. In these cases, it's up to the group (or GM) to determine the order in which they act. Below are a few recommended approaches‚Äîchoose the one that fits your group's pace and tone best:

  • Declared Intent: Ask players to briefly state what their characters intend to do. Let the GM choose who acts first based on narrative flow, momentum, or relevance. This is the default method recommended for most scenes.
  • Highest Relevant Aptitude: The character with the highest relevant Aptitude (e.g. Agility for combat maneuvers, Insight for negotiation) takes the lead.
  • Die Roll-Off: Each player rolls a d6. The highest roll goes first. Ties can be rerolled.
  • Fixed Party Order: The group agrees on a consistent clockwise or seating-based order for players in the same Stance. Fast and frictionless.
  • GM Fiat: For scenes with high tension or narrative weight, the GM can dictate the most cinematic or tactically appropriate sequence.

As always, consistency helps. Once your group agrees on a method, stick to it unless a situation clearly calls for improvisation.

Making It Flow ‚Äî Running Stances Smoothly

While the Stance system offers deep tactical choices, it can feel slower than traditional initiative rolls ‚Äî especially during a group‚Äôs first few sessions. To keep the pace tight and engaging, Seeker encourages the use of quick physical tools and a clear round structure. Below are two techniques that make Stance-based rounds smoother and more immersive.

Stance Cards

Each player receives a set of Stance Cards, one for each of the six core stances (Aggressive, Defensive, Evasive, Balanced, Mystic, Inquisitive). These cards are included in official Seeker materials, but can also be easily printed or written on index cards.

At the beginning of each round, after the GM announces ‚ÄúChoose your Stance,‚Äù all players place their selected card face-down on the table. Once everyone is ready, the GM signals to reveal all cards simultaneously. The round then proceeds according to the declared stances.

This method adds a tactile and visual element to the system, making the moment of choice feel deliberate and dramatic ‚Äî while also preventing players from metagaming based on others‚Äô decisions.

Calling Initiative

After all Stances are revealed, there's no need to calculate who goes first. The GM simply calls out each Stance in priority order, following the established list (Aggressive ‚Üí Evasive ‚Üí Balanced ‚Üí Mystic ‚Üí Defensive ‚Üí Inquisitive).

When the GM says, for example, ‚ÄúAggressive Stance!‚Äù, all characters who chose Aggressive act in any agreed order (see Turn Order Resolution for tie-breaking options). Once they‚Äôve finished, the GM calls ‚ÄúEvasive Stance!‚Äù, and so on, until all turns are complete.

This approach eliminates unnecessary sorting or comparison between characters and keeps the focus on action. It also gives the GM a natural rhythm for guiding the flow of combat.
