Aptitudes

In Seeker, every meaningful action a character takes ‚Äî whether leaping over a chasm, resisting an ancient curse, or negotiating with a wary native tribe ‚Äî is rooted in one of their core Aptitudes.

Aptitudes represent the essential physical, mental, and social capabilities of a Seeker. Rather than relying on long lists of predefined skills, Seeker distills character ability into nine core attributes, each one reflecting a broad aspect of how a character interacts with the world.

Each action, challenge, or conflict in the game is framed through one or more Aptitudes. Whether a character excels through brute Force, clever improvisation, or careful observation, their Aptitudes define how they attempt to shape their surroundings ‚Äî and how well they are likely to succeed.

The Nine Aptitudes

Aptitudes are grouped into three thematic categories. Each time a character performs an action with uncertain or meaningful consequences, the GM selects the most relevant Aptitude based on what the character is doing and how they‚Äôre doing it.

Strength

Raw physical power, explosive force, and physical dominance.

  • Breaking, smashing, pushing, or lifting heavy objects
  • Grappling or overpowering an opponent
  • Wielding large or powerful weapons

Agility

Speed, reflexes, balance, and physical finesse.

  • Dodging attacks or hazards
  • Moving stealthily, jumping, climbing
  • Performing quick, precise motions under pressure

Vigor

Endurance, stamina, and resistance to fatigue or harm.

  • Resisting disease, poison, or prolonged hardship
  • Pushing the body beyond normal limits
  • Enduring pain, harsh weather, or exhaustion

Perception

Awareness of surroundings, sensory focus, and vigilance.

  • Noticing hidden objects, traps, or movement
  • Detecting lies or subtle changes in environment
  • Spotting weaknesses or important details

Intellect

Logic, memory, theoretical knowledge, and learning.

  • Deciphering codes, symbols, or languages
  • Recalling lore, past events, or academic knowledge
  • Analyzing puzzles, problems, or strategies

Adaptability

Improvisation, ingenuity, and creative thinking under pressure.

  • Solving problems using unconventional means
  • Bluffing, adapting to a new culture, or mimicking behavior
  • Making snap judgments with limited information

  • Resisting fear, magic, or emotional control
  • Maintaining focus under stress
  • Fighting temptation, addiction, or corruption
