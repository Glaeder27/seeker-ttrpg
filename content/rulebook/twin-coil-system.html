The Twin Coil System is a lightweight, modular dice‚Äëresolution mechanic that produces non‚Äëbinary outcomes by balancing a character‚Äôs intent against the risk inherent in the world.

Every roll intertwines two opposing forces:

Dice Structure

In the Twin Coil System, all actions are resolved using standard six-sided dice (d6). These are divided into two distinct pools: Action Dice, representing the character‚Äôs proactive effort, and Risk Dice, representing external threats and complications.

Die Symbol Name Description
d6 Action Dice Rolled by the player; measure competence and intent.
d6 Risk Dice Rolled by the GM (or procedure); measure external danger.

Rolling the Dice

To resolve an action, the player and GM each build their respective dice pools. The number of dice reflects both the character's capability and the environmental risk.

  1. Build the Pools
    ‚Ä¢ The player rolls a number of Action Dice equal to their relevant attribute, skill, or trait.
    ‚Ä¢ The GM rolls a number of Risk Dice equal to the situation‚Äôs threat level.
  2. Roll Simultaneously.
  3. Interpret Results:

Tips for GMs

  • Set expectations clearly: Let players know that complications aren't just failures‚Äîthey're story seeds.
  • Use Risk Dice liberally: Especially when you want tension, pacing, or unpredictability.
  • Tie complications to consequences: Make sure they feel meaningful, not just punishing.
  • Don‚Äôt roll for the impossible: If an action is simply out of reach for the character, don‚Äôt roll‚Äîjust describe the failure.
  • Reward creativity: If players find clever ways to reduce Risk Dice (e.g. planning, gear, time), let them.

The GM assigns Risk Dice to represent the potential for unintended consequences during an action. These complications are independent from the action's success and reflect how precarious, unstable, or dangerous the situation is.
