From XP to Levels ‚∏∫ ‚ú¶ ‚∏∫

Each Track‚ÄîCombat, Social, or Exploration‚Äîhas its own XP progression. Every 5 XP earned in a given Track results in a Level Up within that domain. Progress is tracked separately for each Track, reflecting a character‚Äôs specific areas of activity.

Maximum Level Cap

The maximum Level a character can reach in any single Track is Level 10. This represents the pinnacle of skill and influence within that domain, and typically marks the end point for progression in that area during the campaign.

Asymmetrical Growth

Characters in Seeker are meant to grow unevenly, based on the actions they take and the situations they prioritize. It‚Äôs common to see characters with highly divergent progression‚Äîfor example, Level 4 in Exploration, Level 3 in Combat, and Level 1 in Social. This asymmetry reflects organic, choice-driven development rather than forced balance.

Respec & Retraining ‚∏∫ ‚ú¶ ‚∏∫

Characters evolve. After a major narrative event (months of study, a crisis of faith, etc.), the GM may allow a character to trade one advancement for another in the same tier.

This process‚Äîknown as retraining‚Äîrepresents deliberate change, whether prompted by spiritual revelation, physical hardship, or shifting loyalties. It‚Äôs not casual or frequent, but it reflects the dynamic nature of Seekers as they respond to the world.

When to Allow Retraining

Respec is a narrative milestone, not a transactional mechanic. It should feel earned. Some examples include:

  • The character has spent weeks studying with a new mentor or tradition.
  • A traumatic event changes the character‚Äôs beliefs or fighting style.
  • The group enters a new region that demands different aptitudes.
  • The character voluntarily retires a philosophy, faction, or role.

Retraining should not happen during short rests or between minor scenes. The change should be justified through roleplay and integrated into the story.

What Can Be Retrained

Players may trade one advancement for another of the same type, within the same Track and Tier. For example:

  • Swap a Social Skill like Support for another like Influence.
  • Replace a Combat Mastery with a different one.
  • Exchange +1 to an Aptitude for +1 to another within that Track.
Important: Players cannot respec across Tracks (e.g., trading a Combat Skill for a Social Skill), nor change their total XP.

GM Guidance

As GM, you are the final judge of when a retraining opportunity is appropriate. Use it to:

  • Reflect character growth or internal conflict.
  • Reinforce consequences of major narrative beats.
  • Keep players engaged if they regret a previous build choice.

Optional: Consider requiring a short side quest, cost, or ritual to mark the transition‚Äîespecially if the change affects faction ties or magical philosophies.
