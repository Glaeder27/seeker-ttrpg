TL;DR — The Twin Coil System

  • Roll Action vs Risk Dice:
    Use Action Dice (AD) to determine success.
    Use Risk Dice (RD) to determine complications.
  • Success:
    Any AD showing 5–6 = success. One is enough.
    More = better, if using Degrees of Success (optional).
  • Complication:
    Any RD showing 5–6 = complication. They can happen even on success.
    More = worse, if using Degrees of Complication (optional).
  • When to Roll:
    Roll only if both failure and complication are interesting.
    Don’t roll for impossible or trivial actions — just narrate.
  • Risk Dice Benchmarks:
    1 RD = mild risk (~17%)
    2 RD = meaningful risk (~30%)
    3 RD = serious risk (~45%)
    4+ RD = high/catastrophic risk
  • Optional Rules:
    • Degrees of Success: 1 = basic, 2 = strong, 3+ = critical
    • Degrees of Complication: 1 = minor, 2 = serious, 3+ = catastrophic
    • Push: Reroll 1 AD by spending a resource or adding 1 RD
  • GM Tips:
    Gate rolls wisely. Use Risk to build tension. Make complications meaningful. Let the fiction lead.
