TL;DR — The Twin Coil System

▸
  • 1 • Roll Action vs Risk Dice
    Use Action Dice to determine success.
    Use Risk Dice to determine complications.
  • 2 • How to Roll
    Roll a number of equal to the relevant trait or skill modifier for your character.
    are assigned by the GM based on the situation’s danger, urgency, or instability.
    Rolling Risk Dice can be done by the GM or the player — decide as a group what feels more natural.
  • 3 • Resolution
    • 3a • Success
      Any showing = success.
      One is enough.
    • 3b • Complication
      Any showing = complication. They can happen even on success.
  • 4 • When to Roll
    Roll only if both failure and complication are interesting.
    Don’t roll for impossible or trivial actions — just narrate.
  • 5 • Risk Dice Benchmarks 1 → mild risk (~17%)
    2 → meaningful risk (~30%)
    3 → serious risk (~45%)
    4+ → high/catastrophic risk
  • 6 • Optional Rules
    • Degrees of Success: 1 = basic, 2 = strong, 3+ = critical
    • Degrees of Complication: 1 = minor, 2 = serious, 3+ = catastrophic
    • Push: Reroll 1 by spending a resource or adding 1
  • 7 • GM Tips
    • Gate rolls wisely.
    • Use to build tension.
    • Make complications meaningful.
    • Let the fiction lead.
